## Triage notes

**Emerging clusters** (the 10 items are not independent — they group):
- **A. Selection affordance (#1 text, #9 connectors).** The single most-repeated complaint: "I can't tell what's selected." Spans element types → fix at the root (a stronger, consistent selected-state treatment across text / connectors / nodes), not per-type patches. Highest-confidence corroboration expected from the persona sweep (H1/H10).
- **B. Connector hit-region (#5, #3).** Adjacent-tile clicks grab the connector; residual halo after #54. #3 likely folds in. One defect.
- **C. Style-strip behavior & reach (#7 bulk, #8 sticky-vs-default, #10 Shift-select, #11 bulk label font-size).** All touch how the strip + selection model behave. #7/#10/#11 are wiring/convention gaps with existing machinery (#11 folds into #7 + a step-up/down size control); #8 is a genuine **convention decision** (sticky vs default) where the persona sweep may disagree with the owner — resolve with data.
- **D. Model/design (#4 decouple name↔label).** ADR 0032 amendment lane, not the bug lane.
- **E. Native-menu leak (#6).** Standalone bug.

**Routing:**
- **Bug lane (this session candidates):** A (#1/#9), B (#5/#3), #6, plus the cheap wiring fixes #7 and #10.
- **Decision lane (needs owner sign-off, possibly with sweep data):** #8 (sticky vs default), #4 (ADR 0032 amendment).
